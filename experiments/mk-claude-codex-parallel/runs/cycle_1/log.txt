Cycle 1 — mk-claude-codex-parallel

Baseline (3 tasks): speedup 3.00x, 3/3 ok.

Perturbation 1: single task
  parallel=0.20s serial=0.20s speedup=1.00x — degenerate case clean.

Perturbation 2: 10 tasks
  parallel=0.20s serial=2.01s speedup=9.99x — scales linearly with task count
  in the stub regime (since each stub sleeps 0.2s).

Perturbation 3: unknown model adapter
  Returns {success: False, error: "unknown model adapter: gemini"} for the bad
  task; the good task in the same batch still succeeds. Aggregate
  success_count = 1/2, no exception escapes.

Measurement: speedup factor scales linearly with task count under stubs.
Real-world ceiling will be the slowest task in the batch; with two models
and unbalanced task distribution, the speedup peaks at min(N_tasks, 2).
